The Science

Built on measurable change in the brain.

Decision-making is a trainable skill — and the change is physical. Deliberate, structured practice reshapes the brain's pattern-recognition and decision-making networks. Our method is engineered around that: we apply game theory through a demanding strategic environment, then measure the change with our own data. That environment happens to be one of the few with published neuroscience behind it.

01

It reaches the decision networks

Intensive strategic training is associated with stronger connectivity in the brain's cognitive-control and decision-making regions — the prefrontal and cingulate networks our method targets.Chess-expertise neuroimaging review · 2025

02

The change is measurable

Long-term practice shows up as enhanced whole-brain connectivity — described as neuroplasticity from skill acquisition, not inference.Nature · Scientific Reports · 2020

03

It shows up on the scan

Where app-based "brain training" left no measurable trace, this kind of training produces structural and functional change visible on neuroimaging.Peer-reviewed neuroimaging literature
